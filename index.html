Jean Lee is a Ph.D. candidate at the School of Computer Science of the University of Sydney and her research interests include Natural Language Processing and AI applications. With successful research progress over paper writing to top-tier conferences (e.g. ACL, SIGIR, COLING, and AAAI), various projects (e.g. GoogleExploreCSR), and organising a workshop (e.g. Toxic Language Workshop at the AJCAI), she has been selected as a recipient of the Research Training Program (RTP) Scholarship awarded by the Australian Government. She graduated with a Master's Degree in Data Science from the University of Sydney and previously received an MBA from Seoul National University. Prior to academia, Jean has passed the U.S Uniform Certified Public Accountancy Examination (a.k.a. AICPA) and worked in management consulting firms (e.g. Accenture and KPMG) as well as startup industries.
